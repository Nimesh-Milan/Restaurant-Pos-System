Authentication & User Management
4 features

Controls who can access the system and what they can see. This is the foundation — every staff member logs in with their own account, and the system keeps a record of what happens on each shift.

Role-based LoginCore
What it does
Each staff member logs in with their own username and PIN. Admins and cashiers see different screens — cashiers get the ordering and billing tools, while admins also get menu editing, reports, and settings.
Why it matters to you
You control exactly what each staff member can touch. A new cashier can take orders and print bills on day one without being able to change prices or delete menu items by mistake.
Session TimeoutProfessional
What it does
If the till is left untouched for a set period, the screen automatically locks. Whoever wants to use it again has to enter their PIN.
Why it matters to you
Stops a logged-in cashier's session being used by someone else when they step away — especially useful during busy shift changes or when staff walk off to the kitchen mid-order.
Staff Account ManagementCore
What it does
From the admin screen, you can add a new staff account, edit details, or deactivate someone who has left — all without needing a developer to touch anything.
Why it matters to you
High staff turnover is normal in restaurants. When someone leaves, you switch off their account in seconds — no risk of an ex-employee still having access.
Activity LogProfessional
What it does
Every login, logout, order void, and discount applied is recorded with a timestamp and the name of the staff member who did it.
Why it matters to you
If a bill total looks wrong or cash doesn't match at closing, you can trace exactly who did what and when — instead of guessing or accusing staff without proof.

Menu Management
5 features

Everything to do with what you sell — adding dishes, organising them into categories, marking items unavailable, and keeping a record of price changes over time.

Full Menu CRUDCore
What it does
Add, edit, or delete any menu item — name, price, category, and description — directly from the system. CRUD means Create, Read, Update, Delete: the four basic things you need to manage your menu.
Why it matters to you
When you add a new dish or change a price, it updates instantly across every till in the restaurant — no reprinting menus or retraining cashiers on new item codes.
CategoriesCore
What it does
Menu items are grouped into categories like Rice, Kottu, Drinks, and Desserts, shown as tabs on the ordering screen.
Why it matters to you
Cashiers find items faster during busy periods because they're not scrolling through one long list — they tap the category tab first, then the item.
Item PhotosWow Factor
What it does
Upload a photo for any menu item. The photo appears on the ordering screen next to the item name and price.
Why it matters to you
New or part-time staff can recognise dishes visually instead of memorising names — useful when you have a large or frequently-changing menu, and it makes the system feel more polished for anyone watching the screen.
Sold Out ToggleProfessional
What it does
With one tap, mark any item as unavailable for the day. It greys out on the ordering screen so it can't be selected.
Why it matters to you
If the kitchen runs out of an ingredient mid-shift, you stop staff from taking orders for that dish — avoiding the awkward "sorry, that's finished" conversation after the order's already placed.
Price History LogProfessional
What it does
Every time a menu item's price is changed, the system records the old price, new price, date, and who made the change.
Why it matters to you
Gives you a clear record if a customer disputes a price, or if you want to review how your pricing has changed over the months — useful for planning future price increases.

Order Management
6 features

How orders are taken, tracked, and managed across your dining room — from the moment a customer sits down to the moment they're ready to pay.

Table-Based OrderingCore
What it does
Cashiers select a table, add items with quantities, and the order is tied to that table. Multiple tables can have open orders at the same time.
Why it matters to you
This is the core of how a restaurant works — different tables ordering at different times, all tracked separately, so nothing gets mixed up between customers.
Visual Table MapWow Factor
What it does
A floor-plan view of your restaurant where each table shows its live status — free (green), occupied (red/amber), or ready for billing — at a glance.
Why it matters to you
Whoever's managing the floor can see the whole restaurant's status in one screen — which tables need attention, which are ready to pay, and which are free for new customers — without walking around to check.
Order Modification & VoidProfessional
What it does
Items can be edited or removed from an active order. Any removal (void) requires a reason, and it's logged with the staff member's name.
Why it matters to you
Mistakes happen — a customer changes their mind, or a cashier taps the wrong item. This lets staff fix it properly, while giving you a record so voids can't be used to quietly remove paid items from the till.
Item NotesProfessional
What it does
Cashiers can add a short note to any item in an order — like "no chilli" or "extra sauce" — and it prints on the kitchen receipt.
Why it matters to you
Special requests reach the kitchen in writing instead of being shouted across the restaurant or forgotten — fewer remade dishes and fewer unhappy customers.
Table TransferWow Factor
What it does
An active order can be moved from one table to another with a couple of taps — all items, quantities, and notes move with it.
Why it matters to you
Customers sometimes ask to move to a different table mid-meal — maybe for more space, shade, or a better view. Instead of starting the order over, staff just transfer it.
Hold & Recall OrdersProfessional
What it does
An incomplete order can be "parked" and brought back later without losing any items already added.
Why it matters to you
Useful when a customer says "give us a few more minutes" before finalising their order, or when a cashier gets interrupted — nothing is lost, and they can pick up exactly where they left off.

Billing & Payments
6 features

Turns a finished order into a printed bill, takes payment, and keeps a record of every transaction.

Auto Bill GenerationCore
What it does
With one tap, the system generates a full itemised bill — every item, the subtotal, tax, and the final total — calculated automatically.
Why it matters to you
No manual addition, no calculator, no arithmetic mistakes on busy nights. The bill is accurate every single time.
Thermal Receipt PrintingCore
What it does
The bill prints directly to a standard 80mm thermal receipt printer — the same type used at most shops and restaurants — with no extra driver software needed.
Why it matters to you
Customers get a proper printed receipt immediately, just like they'd expect anywhere else — and it works with affordable, widely-available printer hardware.
Cash & Card PaymentCore
What it does
Record how the customer paid — cash or card — and for cash payments, the system automatically calculates the change due.
Why it matters to you
No mental maths for change, and every bill is marked as paid with a record of how — useful for end-of-day cash reconciliation.
Discounts & PromosProfessional
What it does
Apply a percentage or fixed-amount discount to a bill. A reason must be entered, and it's logged against the staff member who applied it.
Why it matters to you
You can run promotions or give loyal customers a discount, while keeping control — every discount is recorded, so staff can't quietly give away free food without a trace.
Split BillWow Factor
What it does
Divide one table's bill between multiple customers, so each person can pay their own portion separately.
Why it matters to you
Groups of friends or coworkers splitting a bill is extremely common — this avoids the awkward manual splitting and re-totalling that slows down busy tables.
Bill ReprintProfessional
What it does
Any previous receipt can be found in history and reprinted exactly as it was originally.
Why it matters to you
If a customer loses their receipt or needs a copy for expense claims, staff can reprint it in seconds instead of recreating the bill from memory.

Inventory & Stock
4 features

Keeps track of the ingredients and supplies you have on hand, warns you before you run out, and records who's changed stock levels and why.

Stock Item ManagementCore
What it does
Add ingredients and supplies to the system with their current stock level, unit of measurement (kg, litres, packs etc.), and a minimum level you want to maintain.
Why it matters to you
You get a single, accurate view of what's in your stockroom — instead of relying on someone's memory or a notebook that's easy to lose track of.
Auto Stock DeductionWow Factor
What it does
When a dish is ordered, the ingredients linked to that dish are automatically subtracted from your stock levels — no manual stock entry needed.
Why it matters to you
Your stock numbers stay accurate in real time without anyone having to count and update manually after every order — a big time-saver as order volume grows.
Stock Adjustment LogProfessional
What it does
Any time someone manually changes a stock quantity — for example after a delivery or to correct a count — it's logged with the reason, quantity, and staff name.
Why it matters to you
If stock numbers don't add up at month-end, you can trace back through the adjustment history to find out where and why.
Supplier DirectoryProfessional
What it does
Store supplier names, contact details, and which stock items each supplier provides.
Why it matters to you
When you get a low-stock alert, the supplier's contact details are right there — no digging through old invoices or phone contacts to find who to call.

Reports & Analytics
7 features

Turns your daily transactions into numbers you can actually use — for managing staff, planning purchases, talking to your accountant, and understanding what's actually making you money.

Daily / Weekly / Monthly RevenueCore
What it does
View total sales, number of orders, and average bill value for any date range you choose.
Why it matters to you
You always know how the business is doing — whether today was better or worse than last week, and whether your average customer is spending more or less over time.
Best-Selling ItemsCore
What it does
A ranked list of your dishes by how many were sold and how much revenue they generated, for any time period.
Why it matters to you
You know exactly which dishes to keep promoting, which ingredients to stock more of, and which underperforming items might be worth dropping from the menu.
Peak Hours HeatmapWow Factor
What it does
A visual grid showing which hours and days of the week are busiest, based on your actual order history.
Why it matters to you
Helps you schedule staff where and when they're actually needed — more hands during your real busy periods, fewer idle staff during quiet ones.
Cashier PerformanceProfessional
What it does
See how many orders each staff member handled, how many discounts they gave, and how many voids they made.
Why it matters to you
Gives you an objective way to recognise your best staff and spot unusual patterns — like one cashier consistently giving far more discounts or voids than everyone else.
End-of-Day Closing ReportProfessional
What it does
One click produces a printable daily summary: total cash collected, card payments, discounts given, and voids made.
Why it matters to you
Closing up at the end of the night takes minutes instead of manually adding up receipts — and the cash drawer can be checked against the report immediately.
Expense TrackingProfessional
What it does
Log day-to-day expenses — gas, ingredient purchases, wages, repairs — directly in the system.
Why it matters to you
Your reports show real profit (revenue minus expenses), not just total sales — giving you a far more honest picture of how the business is actually performing.
Export to PDF & CSVWow Factor
What it does
Any report can be exported as a printable PDF or as a CSV file that opens in Excel or Google Sheets.
Why it matters to you
Makes life easier when you need to hand financial data to your accountant, apply for a loan, or do your own deeper analysis in a spreadsheet.

System & Cloud Sync
5 features

Keeps your data safe, lets you check in on the restaurant remotely, and handles the behind-the-scenes setup and updates so the system stays reliable.

Auto Cloud SyncWow Factor
What it does
Every transaction is automatically sent to a secure cloud server in the background as it happens.
Why it matters to you
You can check sales, reports, and what's happening at the restaurant from your phone or another computer — even if you're not physically there.
Automatic BackupsWow Factor
What it does
The system automatically backs up your data daily — both to a local copy and to the cloud.
Why it matters to you
If the till computer breaks, gets stolen, or has a hard drive failure, your menu, reports, and history can be restored on a new machine in about 30 minutes — instead of losing everything.
Restaurant SettingsProfessional
What it does
Configure your restaurant's name, address, tax rate, and the message printed at the bottom of receipts — all from a settings screen.
Why it matters to you
If your tax rate changes, or you want to add a "Thank you, see you again!" message to receipts, you can update it yourself in seconds — no developer needed.
Update MechanismProfessional
What it does
When the system starts up, it checks if a newer version is available and prompts the admin to update with one click.
Why it matters to you
Bug fixes and improvements reach your system without needing someone to visit in person and manually reinstall software.
Sync Status IndicatorProfessional
What it does
A small indicator on screen shows the last time data was synced to the cloud, with a green, amber, or red status for the connection.
Why it matters to you
At a glance, you or your staff can see if the internet connection is working properly — so if something looks wrong, you know to check the connection rather than assume the system is broken.
